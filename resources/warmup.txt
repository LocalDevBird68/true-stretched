AIMLABS TECHNIQUE

GENERAL

-move mouse only as needed, minimize distance and number of motions of the crosshair.
    -clean, straight lines

-make movements quick while minimizing shakiness and jitter and maintain your accuracy
    -shakiness and jitter is normal with undeveloped tracking

-focus on technique and accuracy

------------------------------------------------------------------

TENSION MANAGEMENT

-use tension sparingly

-avoid death gripping (max tension) but also being fully relaxed (no tension)
    -tension is needed for speed

-use the tension in bursts, release it early during flicks
    -flick kinda looks like it eases into the micro

-micros have low tension and are smooth

-take breaks if anything starts to hurt

------------------------------------------------------------------

TASK-SPECIFIC

-pokeball, hold down ctrl

-read the map, use peripherals for next targets

-set accuracy and score thresholds to train brain


STATIC

-flick fast with a controlled micro correction
    -early tension release + relaxed micro
    -keep lines as straight as possible

-once you start hitting every initial flick, push the speed to get out of comfort zone

-restart if you miss within 10s or on the 4th miss


TRACKING

-react, do not predict

-keep tension low, try to make smooth movements

-assume target wont switch directions, then once it does, follow this:
    -one smooth micro to center or closest edge
        -miss? let it pull ahead
    -back to smooth tracking
    -repeat once it changes directions

-focus on speedmatching and smoothness


DYNAMIC

-fast initial leading flick, relaxed leading micro
    -want to end up where target will be

-do not wait for the bot to enter crosshair for a long time

-focus on accuracy
    -track for like 150ms before shooting
    -try to keep above 80 percent

-dont chase a bot
    -not focusing on one individual bot
    -focus on the next one and flick after clicking
